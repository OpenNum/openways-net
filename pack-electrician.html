Prompt: Kitchen Renovation Electrical Quote

I am a licensed electrician in [state]. A customer in [suburb] wants a quote for kitchen renovation electrical: [list scope, e.g. 5 GPO + 4 LED downlights + rangehood circuit + 2 cooker outlets + safety switch upgrade]. Property age [year built]. Write a written quote covering: 1) scope of work as discrete line items 2) materials list with cable size and circuit notes (no brand pricing) 3) AS/NZS 3000 compliance and CCEW lodgement included 4) labour estimate in hours 5) deposit / balance / warranty terms 6) what is excluded (e.g. tile cutting, cabinetry). Tone: confident tradesman, not salesy. End with a 14-day quote validity line.

Before: 35 minutes per quote, often skipped

"Hi mate — for that kitchen, looking at around $2,800. Can do next week." Sent from the ute. Customer goes silent because there's no scope, no warranty, no compliance line, and the next sparkie sent a real document.

After: 8 minutes from photo to sent

A line-item quote with 5 GPO, 4 LED downlights with circuit notes, rangehood and 2 x 32A cooker outlets, RCBO safety switch upgrade, AS/NZS 3000 + CCEW included, 12 hours labour, 50/50 deposit terms, 12-month workmanship warranty, exclusions listed, 14-day validity. You add the price after the site visit and send. Conversion goes up. Refunds drop.

How to use AI tools in your electrical business

A practical guide for Australian licensed electricians and electrical contractors

Running an electrical business in Australia means balancing on-site work with constant quoting, customer education, compliance communication, and online reputation management. For most sole traders and small electrical contractors, the back-office work happens before and after long days on the tools.

AI tools like ChatGPT and Claude are now used by electricians across Australia to handle customer communications professionally and quickly — without hiring admin staff.

  1. Quote responses. AI produces professional quote replies for residential, commercial, and EV charger jobs — covering scope, timeline, and process clearly.
  2. Customer safety education. Explain switchboard upgrades, safety switch requirements, and EV charger benefits in plain language — prompts designed to build trust, not overwhelm.
  3. Emergency triage replies. When customers contact you with urgent issues, AI helps you respond quickly with the right safety guidance and booking process.
  4. Compliance document support. AI drafts the surrounding communication for compliance certificates — the customer-facing explanation, not the certificate itself (which must be signed by the licence holder).

Why licensed electricians buy this pack

✓
"Won't AI cross the licensing boundary?"
No. Every prompt is designed to draft, scope, summarise or remind — never to specify circuit design or sign off on compliance. The licence holder's judgment and signature still own the wiring, the testing and the CCEW / COES / Certificate of Test. AS/NZS 3000 stays where it should be — under your supervision, not the model's.
✓
"What about customer privacy and address details?"
Real risk — and the pack covers it. Three principles: 1) never paste customer ID, full address, or financial details into a public LLM; 2) use de-identified site descriptions ("3-bed weatherboard, single-phase, 1990s switchboard"); 3) for businesses that handle a lot of sensitive details, upgrade to ChatGPT Team / Claude for Work / Microsoft 365 Copilot (contractual no-training). Original customer data stays in your bookkeeping / job-management app (Xero, MYOB, ServiceM8, Tradify).
✓
"Can AI generate the actual CCEW or COES?"
No — and the pack is explicit about it. CCEW (NSW) lodges through NSW Fair Trading; COES (VIC) goes through Energy Safe Victoria's eCert system; QLD Certificate of Test goes through the Electrical Safety Office. Those are licence-holder declarations on official systems, not chatbot outputs. AI handles the structuring — turning your scrappy site notes into a clean skeleton you can transfer into the official form. You still test, sign and lodge.
✓
"I already use ServiceM8 / Tradify / AroFlo"
Those handle scheduling, time, invoicing and job tracking. This pack handles everything around the work — the quote wording, the emergency triage script, the customer-facing CCEW / COES summaries, the safety education reminders, and the Google Reviews / Xiaohongshu content. The two layers don't overlap; they make each other better.
✓
"My Google Reviews are stuck at 8 — can this help?"
Yes. Electrician keywords (emergency electrician sydney / EV charger installer melbourne / chinese electrician brisbane) are highly review-volume sensitive for local SEO. The pack ships with: post-job invitation templates with photo prompts, bilingual reply templates for 5-star and bad reviews, and a tracking workflow you can run between jobs. Going from 8 to 30 reviews moves you onto page one for most local searches.
