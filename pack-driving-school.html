Prompt: DKT Mock — Wrong-Answer Breakdown in Student's Native Language

My student [name, native language e.g. Mandarin] just sat a DKT mock and scored [X/45]. Wrong questions covered these topics: [right-of-way / roundabouts / speed limits / give-way / school zones — list]. Write me a 250-word breakdown in [native language]: 1) the rule behind each missed topic in plain language 2) the 1-2 trick distractors RMS uses on each topic 3) a 3-day re-drill plan (15 min/day) before next mock 4) one mnemonic per topic the student can use during the real test. Tone: encouraging, not condescending.

Before: 40 minutes per failed mock

"Right, she missed 13 — let me look up which rule each one was about, translate it into Mandarin, think of how to drill it without overwhelming her, type it up in WeChat..." — every failed mock triggers a fresh half-hour of writing from scratch after your last lesson at 8pm.

After: 8 minutes start to send

A 250-word Mandarin breakdown in 3 minutes — each missed rule explained in plain language, the 1-2 RMS trick distractors flagged, a 3-day 15-minute re-drill plan with a mnemonic per topic. You add the in-car observation and tone, then send via WeChat — total time drops to 8 minutes per failed mock.

Why multilingual driving instructors buy this pack

"Won't AI replace my in-car instructor judgement?"
No. Every prompt is designed to draft, organise or translate — never to replace the in-car observation. The licensed instructor's professional judgement and Driving Instructor Licence still own every "ready for test" call. AI handles the WeChat triage, the Mandarin DKT breakdown, the parent weekly report, the test-centre route briefing — you still make the actual go / no-go decision.
✓
"What about student data and parent contact details?"
Real concern — and the pack covers it. Three principles: 1) never paste student licence numbers, full home addresses, or parent banking details into ChatGPT free; 2) use de-identified descriptors ("Student A, 17yo, Mandarin native, lives in Burwood"); 3) for paid sensitive workflows (parent disputes, refund cases), upgrade to ChatGPT Team / Claude for Work (contractual no-training). All confidential student records stay in your booking spreadsheet or paid CRM.
✓
"Can AI accurately predict who'll pass the driving test?"
No — and the pack is explicit about it. The actual pass / fail call belongs to the RMS testing officer, not to AI and not to you. What AI does well: structure your in-car observations into a "ready / not yet" signal, calculate remaining log-book hours, build a route-by-route weakness map for the test centre. What AI does not do: guarantee a pass. Pre-test prediction is a coaching estimate, never sold as a guarantee.
✓
"I already use a booking app / Google Calendar / a paper log book"
Those handle scheduling, payment tracking, and the official 120-hour log book. This pack handles everything around the lessons — bilingual student communication, DKT / HPT breakdowns, weekly parent reports, route analysis, Google Reviews, and Xiaohongshu / WeChat case studies. The two layers don't overlap.
✓
"My Google Reviews are stuck at 9 — can this help?"
Yes. Driving-school keywords (driving instructor sydney / chinese driving school chatswood / nsw driving lessons) are highly sensitive to review volume for local SEO. The pack ships with: post-pass invitation templates (compliant, no incentive language), bilingual reply templates including negative reviews (calm, no defensive blow-back), and a tracking workflow. 15 reviews can move you into the top 3 for chinese-keyword searches in your suburb.
