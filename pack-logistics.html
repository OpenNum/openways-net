How to use AI tools in your freight and logistics business

A practical guide for Australian freight forwarders and customs brokers

Freight forwarding and customs broking in Australia involves constant client communication — shipment status updates, customs documentation explanations, AQIS biosecurity requirements, and Incoterms education. For most SME operators, this communication volume is significant overhead on top of the operational work.

AI tools like ChatGPT and Claude are being used by Australian freight businesses to handle routine client communications professionally and quickly — while staying within ABF/DAFF regulatory boundaries.

  1. Shipment status updates. Professional client updates at each stage of the shipment journey — departure, transit, customs clearance, delivery — drafted consistently every time.
  2. Incoterms education. Explain responsibility, risk, and cost allocation to importers and exporters in plain language — with a general information disclaimer, not a customs declaration.
  3. AQIS biosecurity communications. Clear client-facing explanations of Australian biosecurity hold requirements, treatment options, and timelines — reducing anxious inquiry calls.
  4. Cargo damage and claims. Professional claim notification letters and communication sequences drafted consistently — protecting your relationships through a difficult process.
  5. New client onboarding. Explain your services, documentation requirements, and process clearly to new importers and exporters — in English or Mandarin.

The Logistics AI Pack gives you the prompts and workflows built for Australian freight forwarders — covering FCL, LCL, air freight, and cross-border eCommerce.

All confidential client originals stay in your freight forwarding system / CRM.
