Prompt: Opposing Solicitor Letter — Chinese Client Summary

I represent a client in [case type, e.g. family law property settlement]. The opposing solicitor just sent a [page count]-page English letter, content: [key claims or demands]. Summarise into a 250-word Chinese briefing for my client: 1) opposing core demands (max 2) 2) facts/legal reasoning relied on (plain language) 3) deadline they set and consequence 4) our preliminary response directions (give 2 options with pros/cons) 5) what client needs to do next. Tone: stable, neither alarming nor downplaying.

Before: 60 minutes per opposing letter

"Right, opposing wants 60/40, deadline 14 days... let me think about how to explain this in Chinese... what are our options... how do I phrase this without alarming her..." — every long letter triggers a fresh hour of writing from scratch.

After: 12 minutes start to send

A 250-word Chinese briefing in 5 minutes — opposing 60/40 demand decoded, two response directions with pros/cons mapped, the 14-day deadline calculated as a specific calendar date, three concrete actions for the client. You add the legal judgment and sign off — total time drops to 12 minutes.

Why Chinese-speaking solicitors buy this pack

✓
"Won't AI cross the Solicitors' Conduct Rules boundary?"
No. Every prompt is designed to draft, organise or translate — never to give legal advice. The admitted solicitor's professional judgment and signature still own every decision. Conduct Rule 7 (responsibility for legal services), Rule 9 (confidentiality), Rule 13 (completion of legal work) are baked into every workflow boundary.
✓
"What about client legal professional privilege?"
Real risk — and the pack covers it. Three principles: 1) never paste client ID numbers, passport numbers, home addresses, TFN, or banking details; 2) use de-identified descriptions ("Client A, family law property settlement"); 3) for sensitive matters, upgrade to ChatGPT Team / Claude for Work / Microsoft 365 Copilot (contractual no-training). All confidential client originals stay in your firm PMS and Trust Account systems.
✓
"Can AI translation be used in court?"
No — and the pack is explicit about it. NCAT, Local Court, and Supreme Court require NAATI certified translators with affidavit / statutory declaration for evidence. AI handles the structuring (Chinese narrative → statement of facts skeleton, English orders → client briefing) and the NAATI translator brief. The actual evidence translation goes through NAATI; you do the final legal review.
✓
"I already use LEAP / NetDocuments / Actionstep"
Those handle file management, time recording, Trust Account, and matter lifecycle. This pack handles everything around the matter — bilingual client communication, opposing letter briefings, weekly status reports, Google Reviews, and Xiaohongshu / WeChat case studies. The two layers don't overlap.
✓
"My Google Reviews are stuck at 6 — can this help?"
Yes. Lawyer keywords (chinese lawyer sydney / migration lawyer chinese / family lawyer chatswood) are highly sensitive to review volume for local SEO. The pack ships with: post-closure invitation templates (Conduct Rule 36 compliant — no incentive language), bilingual reply templates including negative reviews (Rule 9 — never publicly discuss case specifics), and a tracking workflow. 10 reviews can move you into the top 3 for chinese-keyword searches.
