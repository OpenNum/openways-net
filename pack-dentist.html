Prompt: Treatment Plan Storyboard — Chinese Patient Explanation

I am explaining a treatment plan to a Mandarin-speaking patient. Diagnosis: [e.g. periapical lesion on tooth 36, RCT recommended, crown to follow]. Build a 250-word Chinese explanation storyboard: 1) what the diagnosis means (plain language, no jargon) 2) what each visit will involve (visit 1 / visit 2 / visit 3) 3) expected total cost range and HICAPS instant-claim estimate 4) Medicare CDBS eligibility check (if under 18) 5) finance plan options (Afterpay / health fund / payment plan). How to use AI tools in your dental practice

A practical guide for Australian dentists and dental clinics

Patient communication in a dental practice is extensive — recall messages, treatment plan follow-ups, appointment confirmations, review requests, and social media. For most practice owners and principal dentists, this communication load falls outside clinical hours but takes hours every week.

AI tools like ChatGPT and Claude are now used by dental practices across Australia to handle routine communications at scale — while staying within AHPRA Advertising Guidelines and Health Records Act boundaries.

  1. Patient recall and reactivation. AI drafts recall messages for overdue patients — personalised, professional, and designed to convert. Bilingual versions for Chinese-speaking patients included.
  2. Treatment plan communication. Explain treatment stages, costs, and next steps to patients in plain language — in English or Mandarin — so they understand and feel confident.
  3. Social media content. AHPRA-compliant educational content for Instagram and Facebook — no testimonials, no prohibited claims, just educational posts that build trust and visibility.
  4. Google review responses. Reply to every patient review — positive or negative — in under 2 minutes, professionally and in compliance with advertising rules.
  5. Finance plan explanations. Help patients understand payment options clearly, in both English and Chinese, reducing drop-off at the treatment acceptance stage.
